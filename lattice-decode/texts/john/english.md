# John 1:1–18 — Plain English

---

**Verse 1.** Before anything existed, there was the logos. Before creation started, the logos was already there. It was oriented toward God — not just beside God, but facing God, turned toward the source. And it shared the nature of God.

**Verse 2.** It was there, oriented toward God, from the very beginning.

**Verse 3.** Everything that exists came into being through it. Nothing came into being apart from it. The logos is the channel through which everything was made. There is no exception.

**Verse 4.** Inside the logos was life. And that life was the light of human beings. Life and light are the same thing, seen from two angles.

**Verse 5.** The light shines in the darkness — and it still shines, right now. The darkness has not overcome it. The darkness cannot overpower it, and cannot even understand it.

---

**Verse 6.** A man appeared — he came into being, as all created things do — sent from God. His name was John.

**Verse 7.** He came as a witness. His job was to testify about the light, so that through his testimony people might come to trust.

**Verse 8.** He was not the light. He was a witness to the light.

---

**Verse 9.** The true light — the one that illuminates every person — was coming into the world.

**Verse 10.** It was in the world. The world came into being through it. And the world did not recognize it.

**Verse 11.** It came to its own — its own domain, its own people. And its own people did not receive it.

**Verse 12.** But as many as did receive it, as many as trusted in its name — to them it gave the right to become children of God.

**Verse 13.** These were not born from biology, or from physical desire, or from any human decision. They were born from God.

---

**Verse 14.** And the logos became flesh.

It took up residence among us — pitched its tent, dwelled here. And we looked at it, we really looked, and we saw its glory. The glory of the one and only, coming from the Father. Full of grace and truth.

**Verse 15.** John testified about it. He said: "The one coming after me ranks ahead of me, because he existed before me."

**Verse 16.** Out of his fullness we have all received — grace upon grace.

**Verse 17.** The law was given through Moses. Grace and truth came into being through Jesus Christ.

**Verse 18.** No one has ever seen God. The one and only — God himself, who is in the closest possible intimacy with the Father — he has made God known. He has decoded him. He has led him out into the open.

---

## What this says, simply

Before anything was made, there was a pattern. It was oriented toward the source. It shared the nature of the source. Everything that exists was made through it — nothing was made without it.

Inside that pattern was life. That life was the light of human beings. The light shines in the dark and the dark cannot put it out.

A man named John came to point to the light. He was not the light.

The light came into the world it had made. The world did not recognize it. Its own people turned it away. But some received it — and those became children of God, born not from human effort but from God directly.

Then the pattern became a human being. It moved in among us. We saw it. It was full of gift and clarity.

Moses gave the law. Jesus Christ brought grace and truth into being.

No one has ever seen God directly. The logos — now flesh, now named — has made him known.

The prologue begins with the logos facing God, and ends with the logos inside the heart of the Father. That is the arc: orientation becoming intimacy.
