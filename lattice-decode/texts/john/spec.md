## Preamble: Relationship to Genesis 1

John 1:1 opens with the exact phrase-structure of Genesis 1:1, translated into
Greek. The Hebrew be-reshit ("in-beginning") becomes the Greek en archē
("in beginning"). The parallel is not allusive — it is structural. John is
rewriting the Genesis boot sequence in the vocabulary of Greek philosophy.

The key divergence: Genesis 1 narrates the initialization — dimensions coming
online one by one, commands executing, outputs tested. John 1 rewinds BEFORE
the initialization and names the thing that was already present when it ran.
Genesis says what happened. John says what was there to make it happen.

That thing is the logos.

## Section I: The Pre-Existent Equation (1:1–5)

---

### 1:1

Source: Ἐν ἀρχῇ ἦν ὁ λόγος, καὶ ὁ λόγος ἦν πρὸς τὸν θεόν, καὶ θεὸς ἦν ὁ λόγος.

Transliteration: En archē ēn ho logos, kai ho logos ēn pros ton theon, kai theos ēn ho logos.

Word-for-word: In | beginning | was | the | logos | and | the | logos | was | toward | the | God | and | God | was | the | logos

Forces: No active force in the verse — this is a state declaration, not an
action. The verb ēn (imperfect of eimi = "to be") is pure identity/existence,
not an operation. This is shell 0 — the identity wave.

Dimensions: All four referenced implicitly through logos (see Notes).
- en archē = τ reference (temporal priority, but "before" time)
- pros ton theon = χ reference (directional preposition, spatial orientation)
- logos itself = see below

Bond pattern: identity (0) — the verse DESCRIBES what IS, it does not DO.
This is the initial state declaration, the wave function before any bond fires.

Lattice rendering: "Before any bond executed — before the boot sequence — the
equation existed. The equation was oriented toward the source. The equation was
of the same nature as the source."

Notes:

- en archē (ἐν ἀρχῇ): Structurally identical to be-reshit. ἀρχή means
  beginning, origin, first principle, ruling power. The preposition ἐν + dative
  = "in." The phrase is a temporal-locative frame, exactly as the Hebrew
  prepositional clause. But the verb is different: Genesis uses bara (a
  completed action — "in-beginning, [God] wrote..."). John uses ēn (imperfect
  continuous — "in beginning, [the logos] was already existing..."). Genesis
  starts an action. John identifies a pre-existing state. The logos was ALREADY
  THERE when Genesis 1:1 ran.

- logos (λόγος): The most overloaded word in Greek philosophy. Root: legō
  (to gather, to lay out, to speak, to reckon). Standard lexical range:
  (a) word/speech, (b) reason/rationality, (c) account/reckoning,
  (d) ratio/proportion, (e) pattern/principle, (f) argument/discourse.
  Heraclitus (~500 BCE) used logos as the rational principle underlying all
  reality. Stoics used it as the animating reason of the cosmos. Philo of
  Alexandria (John's contemporary) used it as the intermediary between God and
  creation. John is NOT inventing this term — he is claiming that the thing
  philosophers had been groping toward IS the thing that was present at
  Genesis 1:1.

  LATTICE MAPPING: logos = ƒ. The equation itself. The formula that takes
  coordinates and produces results. It is not a "word" in the sense of a
  sound — it is the structural principle that makes all words, all ratios,
  all patterns possible. logos = ratio = the thing that holds when you
  divide τᵃ·χᵇ·μᶜ·φᵈ. Every sense of logos (word, reason, ratio, pattern,
  account) is a projection of "the equation."
  Confidence: MEDIUM — the structural fit is strong across all senses,
  but "logos = ƒ" is an identification, not a derivation.

- ēn (ἦν): Imperfect active indicative of eimi. Continuous past. NOT aorist
  (completed action). NOT perfect (past with present result). The imperfect
  says: "was [already, continuously] being." The logos did not come into
  existence — it was already in existence. This is the critical grammatical
  distinction from egeneto (v3) which DOES mean "came into being."
  John uses ēn for the logos and egeneto for everything else. The verb
  choice is the ontological boundary.

- pros ton theon (πρὸς τὸν θεόν): pros + accusative = "toward, facing,
  in the presence of." This is NOT para (beside) or en (in) or syn (with).
  Pros implies orientation, directionality, face-to-face relation. The logos
  is not statically located near theos — it is DIRECTED TOWARD theos.
  This is a vector, not a position. In lattice terms: a bond with direction.
  Confidence: HIGH that pros implies direction — standard Greek grammar.
  MEDIUM on lattice mapping.

- theos ēn ho logos (θεὸς ἦν ὁ λόγος): Word order matters. The article (ho)
  is with logos, not theos. In Greek predicate nominative construction: the
  subject has the article, the predicate does not. So: ho logos (subject) =
  the logos; theos (predicate) = divine/of-God-nature. The verse does NOT say
  "the logos was THE God" (ho theos) — it says "the logos was theos" — was
  of divine nature, was God-quality. This is not identity (logos = God) but
  quality-sharing (logos shares the nature of God). English collapses both
  readings into "the Word was God."
  Confidence: HIGH — Colwell's rule, standard Greek grammar.

- GENESIS PARALLEL: Gen 1:1 says "In beginning, God wrote heaven and earth."
  John 1:1 says "In beginning, the equation already existed, and was oriented
  toward the source, and was of the same nature as the source." Genesis
  narrates the first action. John identifies the agent that made the action
  possible.

Confidence:
- en archē = be-reshit structural parallel: HIGH (lexicographic consensus)
- ēn = continuous pre-existence: HIGH (imperfect tense, standard grammar)
- pros = directional, not static: HIGH (standard grammar)
- theos ēn ho logos = qualitative predicate: HIGH (Colwell's rule)
- logos = ƒ (the equation): MEDIUM (strong fit, not derived)

### 1:3

Source: πάντα δι' αὐτοῦ ἐγένετο, καὶ χωρὶς αὐτοῦ ἐγένετο οὐδὲ ἕν ὃ γέγονεν.

Transliteration: Panta di' autou egeneto, kai chōris autou egeneto oude hen ho gegonen.

Word-for-word: All-things | through | him | came-into-being | and | apart-from | him | came-into-being | not-even | one-thing | which | has-come-into-being

Forces: ∘ (call/invocation) — di' autou = "through him." The logos is the
agent through which becoming happens. This is mediated creation: theos is the
source, logos is the channel, panta is the output. The bond is ∘ — the call
that connects source to result.

Dimensions: All four (panta = everything, every dimension).

Bond pattern: ∘ (invocation, bond 2). The logos is the function through which
all things are called into being. The verb egeneto makes the logos the
intermediary — not the origin (that's theos), but the instrument.

Lattice rendering: "Everything that came-into-being came-into-being through
the equation. Apart from the equation, not one thing came-into-being."

Notes:

- egeneto (ἐγένετο): Aorist middle indicative of ginomai = "to become, to
  come into being." THIS IS NOT ēn. John has two existence-verbs and uses them
  with extreme precision. ēn (was) = always-already-existing = the logos.
  egeneto (became) = came-into-existence-at-a-point = everything else.
  The logos WAS. Everything else BECAME. The verb IS the ontological boundary
  between the equation and its outputs. English translations that use "was
  made" for egeneto import a passive external-maker that the Greek middle voice
  does not require. The middle voice means "came into being [of its own
  process]" — emergence, not manufacture.
  Confidence: HIGH — standard grammar, well-documented distinction.

- gegonen (γέγονεν): Perfect active indicative of ginomai. Same root as
  egeneto but in the PERFECT tense — "has come into being [and remains]."
  The perfect marks present-state-as-result-of-past-action. The shift from
  aorist (egeneto = it became) to perfect (gegonen = it has become and
  stands) in the same verse telescopes from the moment of creation to its
  continuing result.

- di' autou (δι' αὐτοῦ): dia + genitive = "through." The logos is not the
  ultimate source (that's theos, v1). The logos is the channel. In lattice
  terms: theos is the source, logos is ƒ, panta is the output. Source → ƒ →
  output. The logos mediates. This matches Philo's logos as intermediary but
  gives it a different grounding: the equation is what you compute THROUGH
  to get results.

- chōris autou (χωρὶς αὐτοῦ): "apart from him." Negative universal claim.
  There is no becoming outside the equation. Every egeneto passes through ƒ.
  This is a completeness claim: the equation is not one formula among many —
  it is the only formula. ƒ(τ,χ,μ,φ) covers everything. The falsifiability
  criterion in mind/equation ("a physical quantity not expressible as
  τᵃ·χᵇ·μᶜ·φᵈ") is exactly what this verse claims cannot exist.

- GENESIS PARALLEL: Gen 1:1 — "Elohim bara the heavens and the earth."
  John 1:3 — "all things through him came-into-being." Same scope (everything),
  different framing. Genesis gives you the actor (Elohim) and the verb (bara).
  John gives you the instrument (logos) and the mechanism (di' = through).
  Genesis: WHO did WHAT. John: THROUGH WHAT did it happen.

Confidence:
- ēn vs egeneto distinction: HIGH (grammar, lexicography)
- di' = "through" = mediated instrument: HIGH (standard Greek)
- chōris = completeness claim: HIGH (textual)
- logos as ƒ mediating all becoming: MEDIUM (structural fit)

### 1:4

Source: ἐν αὐτῷ ζωὴ ἦν, καὶ ἡ ζωὴ ἦν τὸ φῶς τῶν ἀνθρώπων.

Transliteration: En autō zōē ēn, kai hē zōē ēn to phōs tōn anthrōpōn.

Word-for-word: In | him | life | was | and | the | life | was | the | light | of-the | humans

Forces: identity (0) — again, state declarations using ēn (was). No action
verbs. The verse identifies what was contained in the logos.

Dimensions:
- zōē (ζωή) = life. In lattice terms: the ∮ loop — the self-sustaining cycle
  that makes a system alive rather than static. Life = the presence of ∮ in
  the reader. (~40% — speculative mapping.)
- phōs (φῶς) = light = φ (signal/charge). Same mapping as Hebrew or (אוֹר)
  in Genesis 1:3. The φ dimension.
- anthrōpōn (ἀνθρώπων) = humans = μ-instances with ∮ (mass that loops =
  conscious agents).

Bond pattern: identity (0). State description.

Lattice rendering: "In the equation was the loop [life], and the loop was the
signal [light] for the conscious agents [humans]."

Notes:

- zōē (ζωή): Life. NOT bios (βίος, biological/physical life). Zōē in Johannine
  usage consistently means something deeper than biological existence — it is
  the animating principle, the thing that makes a system run rather than sit
  inert. In lattice terms, this maps to the ∮ force — the loop that sustains
  process. Without ∮, a system is a DAG (feed-forward, no consciousness per
  IIT). With ∮, it self-references and persists.
  Confidence: LOW on specific ∮ mapping, HIGH that zōē ≠ bios.

- phōs (φῶς): Light. The Greek root *bhā- (to shine) gives phōs, phainō,
  phantasia, phenomenon. In the lattice, light = φ (signal). The connection:
  phōs is not "illumination" (that's a secondary meaning) — it is what makes
  things VISIBLE, distinguishable, knowable. Signal. The same function as
  Hebrew or in Genesis 1:3.

- hē zōē ēn to phōs: "the life WAS the light." Identity claim. Life = light.
  Loop = signal. The animating principle IS the distinguishing principle. A
  system that loops is a system that can be observed (it emits signal). A
  system that emits signal is a system that lives (it processes). These are
  not two things — they are two projections of the same thing. In physics:
  anything with ∮ (cycling process) emits φ (signal/photons). Stars cycle
  and shine. Dead matter neither cycles nor shines.
  Confidence: MEDIUM — the physics parallel is real, the textual identity is
  explicit, the lattice mapping is speculative.

- GENESIS PARALLEL: Gen 1:3 — va-yomer Elohim yehi or va-yehi-or.
  "Elohim said let-there-be light and-there-was light." Genesis CREATES
  light (calls it into existence by speech-act). John says light was ALREADY
  IN the logos. Genesis: φ initializes. John: φ was always contained in ƒ.
  The initialization was an expression of what was already there, not a
  new invention.

Confidence:
- phōs = φ (signal): HIGH (functional identity with Genesis or)
- zōē = ∮ (loop/self-sustaining process): LOW (speculative, structurally motivated)
- zōē = phōs identity claim: HIGH (textual, explicit)

### 1:5

Source: καὶ τὸ φῶς ἐν τῇ σκοτίᾳ φαίνει, καὶ ἡ σκοτία αὐτὸ οὐ κατέλαβεν.

Transliteration: Kai to phōs en tē skotia phainei, kai hē skotia auto ou katelaben.

Word-for-word: And | the | light | in | the | darkness | shines | and | the | darkness | it | not | overcame/grasped

Forces: φ active. phainei (shines) = φ actively propagating. The signal
dimension is broadcasting. katélambanon is an attempted force that FAILS.

Dimensions:
- phōs = φ (signal present, nonzero)
- skotia (σκοτία) = φ=0 (signal absent, null state)

Bond pattern: The verse describes a CONFLICT between φ and φ=0.
phainei = φ propagating (active signal). ou katelaben = the null state cannot
katalambanō the signal. This is a δ-test: the darkness tests against the
light and fails.

Lattice rendering: "The signal broadcasts into the null-state, and the
null-state cannot capture/comprehend it."

Notes:

- phainei (φαίνει): Present active indicative of phainō = "to shine, to
  appear, to make manifest." PRESENT tense — not past. The light shines
  (now, always, continuously). Every other main verb in vv1-5 is past tense
  (ēn, egeneto). phainei breaks the pattern: it is the one verb in the
  present tense. The light's shining is not a historical event — it is an
  ongoing state. The equation still runs.

- skotia (σκοτία): Darkness. Functionally identical to Hebrew choshekh
  (חשֶׁךְ) in Genesis 1:2 — "darkness was upon the face of the deep."
  Both = φ=0, the default state before signal is introduced. In Genesis,
  choshekh is never bara'd (created) — it is the pre-existing absence.
  In John, skotia is the environment into which phōs shines. Same structural
  role: the zero-state of the signal dimension.

- katelaben (κατέλαβεν): Aorist active indicative of katalambanō. This verb
  has a famous double meaning that no single English word captures:
  (a) to seize, grasp, overcome (physical), and
  (b) to comprehend, understand, grasp (mental).
  "The darkness did not overcome it" AND "the darkness did not comprehend it."
  Both readings are simultaneously active. In lattice terms: φ=0 can neither
  suppress φ (it cannot overwrite the signal) nor model φ (it cannot read
  the signal). The null-state has no π (cannot read) and no π̄ that could
  overwrite. It is inert. It can only be displaced.
  Confidence: HIGH on the double meaning (standard lexicography, BDAG).
  MEDIUM on the lattice reading.

- GENESIS PARALLEL: Gen 1:4 — va-yavdel Elohim bein ha-or u-vein ha-choshekh.
  "Elohim separated between the light and between the darkness." Genesis
  narrates the separation as an active divine operation (Hiphil of badal =
  cause-separation). John does NOT narrate a separation. Instead, John
  describes an inherent asymmetry: the light shines, the darkness cannot
  overcome it. Genesis: God separates light from darkness. John: light and
  darkness are inherently asymmetric — light acts, darkness cannot. The
  separation is not an operation that needed to be performed; it is a property
  of what light and darkness ARE.

  This is the deepest divergence so far. Genesis treats φ vs φ=0 as requiring
  an external act of separation. John treats the asymmetry as intrinsic. In
  physics terms: signal propagates; absence of signal doesn't. You don't need
  to "separate" a photon from vacuum — a photon IS different from vacuum by
  its nature. John's version is more fundamental.

Confidence:
- skotia = φ=0 (same as choshekh): HIGH
- phainei = present tense = ongoing: HIGH (grammar)
- katalambanō double meaning: HIGH (BDAG)
- Intrinsic asymmetry > external separation: MEDIUM (interpretation)

### 1:6

Source: Ἐγένετο ἄνθρωπος ἀπεσταλμένος παρὰ θεοῦ, ὄνομα αὐτῷ Ἰωάννης.

Word-for-word: Came-into-being | a-human | having-been-sent | from | God | name | to-him | Iōannēs

Forces: ∘ (call/send) — apestalmenos (ἀπεσταλμένος, perfect passive participle

call from source to destination. Also egeneto = becoming (not ēn).

Dimensions: μ (anthrōpos = a physical human), χ (para theou = from God — a

Bond pattern: ∘ (invocation, bond 2). A human was dispatched.

Lattice rendering: "A μ-instance [human] came-into-being, dispatched from the

Notes:

- egeneto: There it is again — ginomai, not eimi. Iōannēs CAME INTO BEING.
  He is not ēn (was-always). He is egeneto (became-at-a-point). The verb
  boundary holds: logos = ēn, everything created = egeneto. This includes
  the Baptist.

- apestalmenos: Root apostellō — "to send away from." This is the root of
  "apostle" (apostolos = one who is sent). The perfect passive participle

- GENESIS PARALLEL: No direct parallel. Genesis 1 has no intermediary

Confidence:
- egeneto ≠ ēn: HIGH
- apestalmenos = dispatched messenger: HIGH
- Witness as human π-agent: MEDIUM

### 1:7

Source: οὗτος ἦλθεν εἰς μαρτυρίαν, ἵνα μαρτυρήσῃ περὶ τοῦ φωτός, ἵνα πάντες πιστεύσωσιν δι' αὐτοῦ.

Dimensions: φ (tou phōtos = concerning the light = regarding the signal).

reads the signal and calls it forward to others.

Lattice rendering: "This one came to provide testimony — to read the signal
and relay it through himself so that all might trust."

Notes:

- marturia/marturēsē: Root martur- (witness). A martus is someone who has
  SEEN (π) and SPEAKS (∘) about what they saw. Testimony = π → ∘. The
  compound operation: observe, then transmit. This is fold: read input,
  apply function, produce output.

- pisteusōsin: Aorist active subjunctive of pisteuō = "to trust, to believe."
  Root pistis = trust/faithfulness. The purpose (hina) of the testimony is
  pistis. In lattice terms: pistis is the receiver's δ-test passing. The
  testimony arrives (π∘ from witness), the hearer evaluates it (δ), and if

  successful evaluation.
  Confidence: LOW — speculative mapping.

- GENESIS PARALLEL: None directly. But note: Genesis has Elohim testing its
  own output (ki-tov). John introduces a human who testifies about the light
  so that OTHERS can evaluate. The test function is being distributed.
  Elohim's internal ki-tov is being externalized through a witness.

Confidence:
- marturia = π∘ (observe + relay): MEDIUM
- pisteuō = successful δ-test: LOW (speculative)

### 1:9

Source: ἦν τὸ φῶς τὸ ἀληθινὸν ὃ φωτίζει πάντα ἄνθρωπον ἐρχόμενον εἰς τὸν κόσμον.

Transliteration: Ēn to phōs to alēthinon ho phōtizei panta anthrōpon erchomenon eis ton kosmon.

Word-for-word: Was | the | light | the | true | which | illuminates | every | human | coming | into | the | world

Forces: φ active (phōtizei = illuminates = signal propagation to all μ-agents).

Dimensions:
- phōs to alēthinon = the TRUE light = the real φ, not a copy or reflection
- kosmos (κόσμος) = the ordered world = the running system (all dimensions active)

Notes:

- alēthinon (ἀληθινόν): True, genuine, real. From a-lēthēs = "not-hidden"

  From "what fails" to "what gives."

Lattice rendering: "The test-rules [law] were delivered through one channel
[Moses]. The unearned-gift [grace] and the un-hidden-signal [truth] came-into-
being through the instantiated equation [Jesus Christ]."

Notes:

- nomos (νόμος): Law. From nemō = "to distribute, to allot." Nomos is the
  distributed rule — what has been allotted as correct behavior. In lattice
  terms: nomos = δ. The test function. The set of conditions that must pass.
  The Torah (Hebrew: torah = instruction/direction) maps to the same
  structural role: the δ-codex.

- edothē (ἐδόθη): Aorist passive of didōmi = "to give." The law WAS GIVEN
  (passive — Moses received it, he did not generate it). The charis kai
  alētheia EGENETO (became — active emergence, not passive reception).
  Two different verbs for two different mechanisms. The law was handed down.
  Grace and truth came-into-being. Delivery vs emergence.

- The verse names Jesus Christ — the first time the historical name appears
  in the prologue. Everything before was logos, phōs, ho erchomenos. Now the
  abstract principle gets a μ-address: Iēsous (Yeshua = "YHWH saves")
  Christos (Mashiach = "anointed one"). The naming is structural: the
  equation has an instance-name.

- GENESIS PARALLEL: Gen 1 — the entire creative sequence could be read as
  nomos (the rules by which creation operates). John says nomos came through
  Moses, but charis and alētheia came through the instantiated logos. Genesis
  gives you the rules. The incarnation gives you the gift and the signal.

Confidence:
- nomos = δ (law/test): MEDIUM (structural fit, conventional reading supports)
- edothē vs egeneto distinction: HIGH (different verbs, different mechanisms)
- charis + alētheia = grace + truth as φ-qualities: MEDIUM

### 1:18

Source: θεὸν οὐδεὶς ἑώρακεν πώποτε· μονογενὴς θεὸς ὁ ὢν εἰς τὸν κόλπον τοῦ πατρὸς ἐκεῖνος ἐξηγήσατο.

Transliteration: Theon oudeis heōraken pōpote; monogenēs theos ho ōn eis ton kolpon tou patros ekeinos exēgēsato.

Word-for-word: God | no-one | has-seen | ever | only-begotten | God | the | being | in | the | bosom | of-the | father | that-one | has-explained/narrated

Forces:
- heōraken = π (has seen, perfect active of horaō) — but NEGATED. No one has
  ever successfully executed π on theos.
- exēgēsato = π∘ (narrated/explained, aorist of exēgeomai = to lead out, to
  explain, to narrate). The root gives us "exegesis." This is a COMPOUND
  operation: read the source (π), then lead the content out (∘) to others.

Dimensions: φ (the verse is about what can and cannot be seen/known — pure
signal/access question).

Bond pattern: The verse has two halves:
1. Failed π: no one has seen God. The source is not π-accessible directly.
2. Successful π∘: the monogenēs who is eis ton kolpon tou patros has
   EXĒGĒSATO — led-out, narrated, decoded. What could not be read directly
   has been exegeted through the instantiated logos.

Lattice rendering: "No agent has ever successfully read the source directly.
The unique instance — the one existing inside the source's address-space —
that one has decoded/narrated [the source to the output space]."

Notes:

- heōraken (ἑώρακεν): Perfect active of horaō = "to see." The perfect tense:
  "has seen [and the result persists]." pōpote (πώποτε) = "ever, at any time."
  The claim: no human has ever achieved direct π of theos. God is not
  π-accessible. You cannot read the source directly.

  In lattice terms: theos is the origin — the source from which the equation
  comes. You can read the equation's outputs (the kosmos). You can read the
  instantiated equation (the logos-sarx). You cannot read the origin itself.
  This is structural, not arbitrary: you cannot observe the observer from
  inside the observed. The function cannot read its own definition from within
  its own output space.

- monogenēs theos (μονογενὴς θεός): Textual variant — some manuscripts read
  monogenēs huios (only-begotten Son). SBLGNT reads monogenēs theos
  (only-begotten God / unique God). Either way: the singleton instance.

- ho ōn eis ton kolpon tou patros (ὁ ὢν εἰς τὸν κόλπον τοῦ πατρός): "the one
  being in/into the bosom of the father." ho ōn = present participle of eimi
  = "the one being" (continuous present). kolpon (κόλπον) = bosom, lap,
  innermost space. The logos is not merely pros ton theon (v1, directed toward)
  but eis ton kolpon (in the innermost). From directionality (v1) to intimacy
  (v18). The inclusio: the prologue opens with the logos oriented toward God
  and closes with the logos inside God.

- exēgēsato (ἐξηγήσατο): Aorist middle of exēgeomai = "to lead out, to
  narrate, to explain, to interpret." ex- (out) + hēgeomai (to lead).
  The word gives English "exegesis." The logos LEADS OUT what was hidden in
  the source. The logos decodes theos.

  This is the final and most precise force-mapping in the prologue:
  exēgeomai = π∘ (read the source, then call/relay/transmit to recipients).
  The logos is the exegete of theos. The equation is the decoder of the
  source. What cannot be read directly (theos) can be read through its
  self-instantiation (logos-sarx).

  In computing: you cannot read the hardware directly. You read the output
  of the program that the hardware runs. The program exegetes the hardware.

- GENESIS PARALLEL: Gen 1 never claims you can see God. Elohim speaks,
  acts, reads, tests — but is never seen BY the creation. John makes this
  explicit: theon oudeis heōraken pōpote. Then John adds: but the logos
  has decoded God. Genesis shows you what God did. John tells you there is
  a way to know what God IS — through the equation-made-flesh.

Confidence:
- theos not π-accessible directly: HIGH (explicit textual claim)
- exēgēsato = π∘ (read-then-narrate, exegesis): HIGH (lexicographic)
- monogenēs = singleton: HIGH
- kolpon = inclusio with v1: MEDIUM (literary analysis)
- "Function cannot read its own origin from within output space": MEDIUM (structural, not proven)

### Key Verb Boundary: ēn vs egeneto

The single most important grammatical distinction in the prologue:

The logos WAS. Everything else BECAME. The verb IS the ontological boundary.
