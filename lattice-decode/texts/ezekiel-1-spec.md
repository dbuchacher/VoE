## Preamble: Structural Overview

Ezekiel 1 is not a creation account. It is a REPORT — a first-person observation
of a running system. Genesis 1 boots the machine. Ezekiel 1 describes the machine
in operation, seen from the outside by a human observer.

The chapter divides into five structural units:
1. Frame (vv. 1-3): Who, where, when — the observer's coordinates
2. The Storm (v. 4): Entry signal — phi burst from the north
3. The Four Chayot (vv. 5-14): Four living creatures — the processing units
4. The Ofanim (vv. 15-21): Wheels within wheels — the loop/transport mechanism
5. The Throne (vv. 22-28): Raqia, sapphire seat, the demut — the model itself

Key term: DEMUT (likeness/model). This word dominates the chapter — it appears
10 times. Ezekiel does not see the thing itself. He sees the demut of the thing.
He sees a MODEL. This is observation through indirection, not direct access.

## CRITICAL ANALYSIS: Do The Four Faces Map to tau, chi, mu, phi?

The four faces: human (adam), lion (aryeh), ox (shor), eagle (nesher).
The four dimensions: tau (time), chi (distance), mu (mass), phi (charge).

Possible mapping attempt:

| Face   | Candidate | Reasoning | Confidence |
|--------|-----------|-----------|------------|
| adam   | tau?      | Human = consciousness = self-reference = temporal being (aware of time, memory, anticipation). Humans are the tau-aware entity. | 35% |
| aryeh  | phi?      | Lion = signal, dominance, roar = phi (charge, signal emission). The lion's primary attribute is its roar (qol) and its mane (visible signal). | 30% |
| shor   | mu?       | Ox = mass, labor, physicality, grinding work. The ox is the mass-carrier, the beast of burden, pure mu. | 45% |
| nesher | chi?      | Eagle = spatial mastery, flight, distance-seeing. The eagle commands chi-space — it sees the farthest, flies the highest. | 40% |

Honest assessment: the ox-to-mu mapping is the strongest. The ox IS mass-
labor-physicality. The eagle-to-chi mapping is next: flight = spatial
dominance. The lion-to-phi mapping is weak: lion could equally be mu
(powerful body) or tau (king of beasts = temporal sovereignty). The
human-to-tau mapping is the weakest: humans touch all four dimensions.

ALTERNATIVE: the four faces might not map to the four dimensions at all.
They might map to the four FORCES:
- adam (human) = pi (read, observe, consciousness)
- aryeh (lion) = delta (test, judge, select — the lion as judge/king)
- shor (ox) = ∮ (loop, labor, grinding cycles — the ox walks in circles)
- nesher (eagle) = ∘ (call, dispatch, messenger — the eagle as courier)

This force mapping is arguably BETTER than the dimension mapping:
- Human reads (consciousness IS observation = pi)
- Lion tests/judges (king evaluates = delta)
- Ox loops (threshing floor = circular labor = ∮)
- Eagle dispatches (messenger, high-perspective scout = ∘)

If this force mapping holds: the four faces on each creature mean each
creature has all four forces. Each creature is a complete processing
unit: it can read (adam), call (nesher), loop (shor), and test (aryeh).
Four creatures x four forces = each creature is a hylo (pi+∘+∮+delta).

Confidence on force mapping: 45% — higher than dimension mapping (35%).
Both speculative. Neither proven.

The tradition (Ezekiel's own later identification in chapter 10, and the
later kabbalistic/Christian tradition) assigns varied interpretations.
The text itself does not explain what the faces mean. Any mapping is
imposed from outside.

  interface, and the eagle is what you see from behind, then the read
  interface (human = pi?) leads and the dispatch interface (eagle = ∘?)
  follows. The lateral faces (lion/ox = delta/∮?) are orthogonal.

  This spatial arrangement maps to a coordinate system: forward = +x,
  right = +y, left = -y, back = -x. Four faces = four cardinal directions

  ground in later verses), so the four faces give them 4 of the 6 possible
  cardinal directions in 3D. The top and bottom are covered by wings
  (above) and feet (below). Six surfaces total. Four faces + 2 wing/foot
  surfaces = six-directional coverage.

Confidence:
