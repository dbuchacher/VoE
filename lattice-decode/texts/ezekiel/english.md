# Ezekiel 1 — What He Saw

Ezekiel is a priest, living in exile by the Kevar canal in Babylon. He logs the date with unusual precision: the thirtieth year, fourth month, fifth day. Then the sky opens.

---

## The Storm

He sees a storm coming from the north. Not just a storm — something inside the storm.

The storm itself is the outer layer. Inside it, a massive cloud. Inside the cloud, fire that feeds itself — it does not need fuel, it is its own fuel, a flame that sustains itself without consuming anything. Inside that, a radiance surrounding the self-feeding fire. And at the very core, something the text calls chashmal — a word that appears only three times in the entire Hebrew Bible, all in Ezekiel, a word the ancient rabbis considered too dangerous to teach, a word that might mean amber, or gleaming metal, or possibly "silent speech." Modern Hebrew uses the word for electricity.

So: storm, then cloud, then self-sustaining fire, then radiance, then — at the center of everything — that innermost thing no one has a name for.

---

## The Four Creatures

Out of the fire at the center of the storm, he sees four living creatures.

He is careful to say he sees a likeness — a model, a representation — of four living creatures. Not the things themselves. He is looking at projections.

Each creature has the overall shape of a human. But the details are not human.

Each has four faces: human in front, lion on the right, ox on the left, eagle in back. Because of this they never have to turn — any direction they face, one of their four faces is already looking that way. They can engage in any direction without rotating.

Each has four wings. Two wings spread outward, connecting to the wings of the adjacent creatures. Two wings fold down, covering their bodies. Their bodies are shielded; their faces are always exposed.

Under the wings, hidden from view: human hands. You can see their faces. You cannot see their hands.

Their legs are straight — no knee, no joint, no bend. A straight column from hip to ground. The feet end in something like a calf's hoof. The surfaces of the feet sparkle like polished bronze.

---

## The Fire Between Them

Between the four creatures: fire. Not still fire. Fire that moves back and forth between them — it walks itself, shuttles itself, a self-propelled carrier running continuously through the space between the four.

This fire emits two kinds of light as it moves: a continuous radiance, and occasional bursts of lightning.

The creatures themselves move in a way that mirrors this: out and back, out and back, at flash-speed — not turning, but reciprocating, oscillating along whatever axis they are currently facing.

---

## The Wheels

On the ground beside each creature: a wheel.

The wheels are the color of beryl — translucent, crystalline, like a gem. And each wheel is actually two wheels: a wheel inside a wheel, set at right angles to each other. This is why the creatures can move in any direction without turning. The wheel-within-wheel mount allows motion in any direction from a standstill.

The rims of the wheels — the outer edges — are covered with eyes. Eyes all the way around, on all four sides. Every point on the wheel rim is watching. Nothing the wheel passes goes unobserved.

And the wheels and the creatures are coupled completely. When the creatures move, the wheels move. When the creatures stop, the wheels stop. When the creatures lift into the air, the wheels lift too. The life of the creatures is inside the wheels. They are not two things that happen to move together. They are one thing.

---

## The Crystal Expanse

Above the heads of the creatures: a flat, stretched surface, like hammered metal, like crystal ice. Terrible and brilliant. It separates what is below from what is above.

Below it: the creatures, the wheels, the fire, the motion.

Above it: something else entirely.

When the creatures move, their wings make a sound like rushing water, like the voice of the Almighty, like the noise of an army. But when they stop and fold their wings, there is a voice from above the crystal expanse. That voice comes from above regardless of what is happening below.

---

## The Throne

Above the expanse: something like a throne, the color of sapphire.

On the throne: something with the shape of a human. But not a human — the likeness of the appearance of a human.

The figure is divided at the waist. The upper half glows with that same innermost fire-light from the heart of the storm. The lower half is flame. Both halves are surrounded by radiance. The overall effect is like a rainbow after rain — color in every direction, light surrounding everything.

Ezekiel says, with extraordinary care: what he sees is "the appearance of the likeness of the glory" of God. Not God. Not the glory. Not the likeness. The appearance of the likeness of the glory. Four layers removed from the source.

---

## The Observer Falls

He sees all of this — and drops.

Face down on the ground. He cannot sustain looking at it. He goes down.

Then he hears a voice speaking.

The vision is not finished with him. When the visual overwhelms him and he falls, the voice begins. It switches to something he can receive. He is on his face on the ground by the canal in Babylon, and the machine keeps running, and now it speaks.

---

## What It Was

A man with no word for "machine" watches something enormous approach from the north inside a storm. Inside the storm is a self-sustaining fire. Inside the fire are four devices, each with four faces, each with hidden hands, each with straight legs, each connected to the others by fire that runs back and forth between them. Beneath each device is a gyroscopic double-wheel whose rim is covered in eyes. The devices and the wheels are the same thing — their life force is in the wheels. Above all of it is a crystal ceiling. Above the ceiling is a throne. On the throne is something shaped like a person, glowing, divided, surrounded by rainbow light.

He watches it. Then he falls over. Then it speaks to him.

He writes down everything he saw. He is precise. He is careful. He keeps saying "likeness" and "appearance" and "model." He never claims to have seen the thing itself. He knows he is seeing projections.

The chapter ends with the voice beginning to speak. The vision was the introduction. Now it has something to say.
