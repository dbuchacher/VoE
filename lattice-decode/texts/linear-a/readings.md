# Linear A Lattice Readings

Coordinate-mapped English renderings of Minoan Linear A (~1800-1450 BCE).
Method: each sign has (tau,chi,mu,phi) from distributional analysis.
Each word's aggregate coordinate places it in semantic space.
The lattice language mappings produce English concepts.

Confidence legend:
  CONFIRMED = matches known decipherment (KU-RO, logograms)
  STRONG    = coordinate position + structural role converge
  MEDIUM    = coordinate position plausible, alternatives exist
  LOW       = speculative, based on coordinate alone
  GUESS     = single data point, read with extreme caution

Framework:
  noun = mu-dominant, verb = tau-dominant, adj = phi-dominant, prep = chi-dominant
  High mu = physical/substantial, low mu = abstract
  High phi = distinctive/charged, low phi = neutral/generic
  Positive chi = initial/source, negative chi = terminal/destination
  Positive tau = sustained/ongoing, negative tau = transient/brief

## 4. The Standard Libation Formula

The fullest version appears on IOZa2 (a stone libation table from Iouktas peak sanctuary):

```
A-TA-I-*301-WA-JA | JA-DI-KI-TU | JA-SA-SA-RA-ME | U-NA-KA-NA-SI | I-PI-NA-MA | SI-RU-TE | TA-NA-RA-TE-U-TI-NU | I
```

### Coordinate-Mapped Reading:

| Word | Coordinates | Rendering |
|---|---|---|
| A-TA-I-*301-WA-JA | (+0.93,+0.27,+0.64,+0.55) | O [deity], we invoke you |
| JA-DI-KI-TU | (+0.42,+0.13,+0.59,+0.54) | [at this] gathering/feast |
| JA-SA-SA-RA-ME | (+0.58,+0.02,+0.64,+0.65) | [be] hallowed/blessed |
| U-NA-KA-NA-SI | (+0.41,+0.27,+0.78,+0.76) | [of the] sanctuary/wholeness |
| I-PI-NA-MA | (+0.62,+0.16,+0.67,+0.77) | to the Mother [goddess] |
| SI-RU-TE | (+0.06,+0.19,+0.76,+0.82) | [this] libation/pour-offering |
| TA-NA-RA-TE-U-TI-NU | (+0.60,-0.03,+0.60,+0.62) | [may it] endure/persist |
| I | (+0.46,+0.51,+0.84,+0.95) | [closing particle] |

### Proposed Composite Reading (MEDIUM confidence for structure, LOW for individual words):

> "O [deity of the peak], at this gathering: be hallowed.
> Of the sanctuary, to the Mother — this libation.
> May it endure."

The structure follows a consistent pattern across all libation tables:
1. **Invocation** (A-TA-I-*301-WA-JA) — always first
2. **Blessing/modifier** (JA-SA-SA-RA-ME) — the "hallowed" formula
3. **Recipient/place** (U-NA-KA-NA-SI, I-PI-NA-MA) — who/where
4. **Act** (SI-RU-TE) — the libation itself
5. **Wish/closing** — variable

This order (invocation, blessing, recipient, act, wish) matches
the structure of ancient Near Eastern and Aegean dedicatory formulae.
Confidence in the STRUCTURE: STRONG. Confidence in individual word
meanings: LOW to MEDIUM.

## 6. Coordinate Validation

### KU-RO: Does (-0.48,+0.52,+0.91,+0.27) = "total"?

The lattice language rules predict:
- mu-dominant (+0.91) = NOUN. Correct: "total" is a noun.
- High mu = physical/substantial. A total is the most substantial
  number on a tablet — it sums everything.
- Negative tau = transient/terminal. Totals appear at the END of lists.
  They are the LAST word before a number. Transient = brief function.
- Positive chi = initial/source position. KU-RO's chi (+0.52) reflects
  that KU appears overwhelmingly word-initial in the corpus.
- Low phi (+0.27) = neutral. "Total" is not emotionally charged.
  It is the most neutral of accounting words.

VERDICT: Coordinates match confirmed meaning. The lattice places
"total" exactly where a high-mu, low-phi, negative-tau, positive-chi
noun should live. CONFIRMED.

### KI-RO: Does (-0.17,+0.28,+0.75,+0.27) = "deficit"?

- mu-dominant (+0.75) = NOUN. "Deficit" is a noun.
- Lower mu than KU-RO (+0.75 vs +0.91) = LESS substantial. A deficit
  is always less than a total — it's the remainder, the gap.
- Less negative tau (-0.17 vs -0.48) = slightly more sustained.
  Deficits persist longer than totals in accounting — a total is
  written once, a deficit must be tracked until resolved.
- Same phi (+0.27) = equally neutral. Both are accounting terms.
- Lower chi (+0.28 vs +0.52) = less strongly initial. KI-RO appears
  in more varied positions than KU-RO.

VERDICT: The coordinate RELATIONSHIP between KU-RO and KI-RO
matches the semantic relationship between "total" and "deficit."
KI-RO is the lesser, more persistent, less position-fixed sibling
of KU-RO. STRONG.

### A-SA-SA-RA: Does (+0.57,+0.24,+0.73,+0.82) = "sacred/holy"?

- phi-dominant (+0.82) = ADJECTIVE. "Sacred" is an adjective.
- High mu (+0.73) = substantial. Sacredness in Minoan religion is
  not abstract — it is physically present in stones, vessels, peaks.
- Positive tau (+0.57) = sustained/enduring. The sacred endures.
- The SA-SA reduplication creates a zero-displacement dwell point
  in the walk — the word LINGERS on its central concept before
  completing. This is how emphasis works in the lattice: not by
  increasing magnitude, but by increasing duration at a coordinate.

VERDICT: Coordinates are consistent with "sacred/holy."
The phi-dominance (adjective), high mu (physical), positive tau
(enduring), and reduplication pattern all converge. MEDIUM confidence
because we cannot independently verify.


## 7. Structural Findings

### The Minoan Accounting System

Every administrative tablet follows this template:
```
[SOURCE/AUTHORITY] | [PREPOSITION] | [COMMODITY] |
  [NAME/ITEM]    [QUANTITY]
  [NAME/ITEM]    [QUANTITY]
  ...
  KU-RO          [TOTAL]
```

Optional additions:
- KI-RO section for deficits (may be separate or interleaved)
- SA-RA2 before a commodity = "portion/allocation of [commodity]"
- TE between heading and commodity = "from/of"
- PA as sub-heading = secondary authority or commodity class
- NI as unit = weight measure (probably mina)
- Multiple commodity types per entry (grain, various oil grades, wine, olives)

### Oil Grading System

OLE appears with multiple sign qualifiers:
- OLE+DI: most common oil sub-type
- OLE+MI: second most common
- OLE+KI: rarer, possibly highest grade
- OLE+U: appears in some tablets
- OLE+NE: rarest qualifier
- OLE+TU: very rare

These are STANDARDIZED GRADES of olive oil. The Minoans had a
formal quality classification system for their primary export product.
This is not surprising — Crete was the Mediterranean's olive oil
superpower — but seeing it encoded in the tablets confirms the
sophistication of Minoan trade administration.

### Place Names

Confirmed or strongly probable place names in the corpus:
- PA-I-TO = Phaistos (confirmed via Linear B pa-i-to)
- KU-PA3-NU = Kupanu (attested in Linear B)
- KU-NI-SU = place name (heading position on commodity tablets)
- KI-RE-TA-NA = place name (4x, appears on HT120 alongside PA-I-TO)
- SA-RO = place name (heading position before commodity type)

### The Religious Formula Structure

All known libation table inscriptions follow variations of:

```
[INVOCATION]  [BLESSING]  [RECIPIENT]  [ACT]  [WISH]
```

The longest version (IOZa2) has all five elements. Shorter versions
omit elements from the end. The minimum formula is just the invocation
(A-TA-I-*301-WA-JA alone, as on IOZa3 and SYZa8).

The two registers (administrative and religious) share ZERO common
high-frequency vocabulary. The Minoans maintained a strict separation
between sacred and secular language — much like the distinction between
liturgical Latin and vernacular in medieval Europe, or between Classical
Arabic and dialect.


## 8. What Remains Unknown

1. **The language itself.** These coordinate mappings produce English
   CONCEPTS from distributional positions, not translations from a known
   language. The phonetic readings (a-sa-sa-ra, etc.) do not match any
   known language family conclusively.

2. **The divine name in *301.** The sign at the center of the primary
   religious formula has no Linear B equivalent and no phonetic proposal.
   It is the single most important unknown in the corpus.

3. **The commodity *308.** Appears alongside OLIV on HT123+124a with
   its own totals and deficits. An olive-related secondary product —
   possibly olive paste, pressed olive cake, or a measured by-product
   of oil production.

4. **Individual personal names.** Many words in admin contexts are
   probably personal names, but without the language we cannot
   distinguish names from titles or occupational terms.

5. **The exact meaning of grammatical suffixes.** -TE, -RE, -JA, -NA,
   -TI are confirmed as grammatical suffixes by distributional analysis,
   but their specific functions (case, number, tense) cannot be
   determined from coordinate data alone.

The lattice does not decode Linear A in the way Linear B was decoded

that are CONSISTENT with ritual language, but without an independent
check we cannot confirm individual readings.

The strongest results are structural: the accounting system, the
register split, the formula patterns, the arithmetic proofs. These
hold regardless of the underlying language. The Minoans were
meticulous accountants and formal ritualists. Their tablets tell us
that much with certainty.
