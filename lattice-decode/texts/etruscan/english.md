# Etruscan Texts — Plain English


## The Pyrgi Tablets

This temple, and this statue/sacred thing, was dedicated to Uni (Juno) of Astarte, in this place, by the people's community. To Thefarie Veliana — [of Clusium?] — [who] gave/dedicated [it], of the crypt, [in] the office-holding, having [performed the act], of the boundaries. Because three years [ago?] ... a statue ... [for] a year ... [at the time of] the stars.

(The Phoenician parallel reads: "For the Lady, for Astarte, this is the holy place, which was made and placed by Tiberius Velianas, king over Kasriye...")


## The Liber Linteus (selected passages)

Offering — whether funerary or chthonic — placed on decorated litter. Speak: "sacrifice this offering and [X]." [X] as [Y] in sacrifice. Libation [performed]. [X] on decorated litter likewise [performed]. Libation [complete].

(Source passage: "[fl]er, etnam tesim, etnam c[elucn], cletram srenxve. trin: thezine chim fler tarc. mutin um anancves; nac cal tarc thezi. vacl an scanince saucsath. persin cletram srenxve ich scanince. clt vacl ara.")


## Common Tomb Inscriptions

### Formula 1 — "This is the tomb"

This is the tomb of Thanxvil, daughter of Mashni.

(Etruscan: eca suthi thanxvilus mashnial)

### Formula 2 — "Lived ... years"

Larth Sentes, son of Sethre, lived 30 years.

(Etruscan: sentes laris sethres svalce avil XXX)
