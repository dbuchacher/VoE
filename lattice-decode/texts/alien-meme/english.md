## Lattice Coordinate Mapping

Mapping visual properties to dimensions:

  τ (duration/complexity): stroke count
    1-2 strokes = transient (-1)
    3 strokes = transitional (0)
    4+ strokes = sustained (+1)

  χ (space/direction): primary orientation
    upward/rightward = positive (+1)
    balanced/symmetric = neutral (0)
    downward/leftward = negative (-1)

  μ (mass/substance): enclosed area
    open (lines only) = light (-1)
    partial enclosure = medium (0)
    circles/closed shapes = heavy (+1)

  φ (charge/signal): symmetry
    bilateral symmetric = positive (+1)
    partial symmetry = neutral (0)
    asymmetric = negative (-1)

## What the Lattice Sees

### Distribution Check: Is This Language?

For a real writing system, we'd expect:
- Zipf distribution (a few very common, long tail of rare) — CAN'T TEST (no text, just inventory)
- Positional preferences — CAN'T TEST (no word structure)
- Bond patterns between adjacent signs — CAN'T TEST (no sequences)

With a bare inventory grid and no text, we can only analyze the SIGN SYSTEM, not a language.

### Sign System Analysis

**Dimension balance:**
  τ: 8 complex (+1), 21 transitional (0), 0 transient (-1) → SKEWED HIGH
  χ: 6 positive, 16 neutral, 8 negative → ROUGHLY BALANCED
  μ: 14 open (-1), 4 partial (0), 12 enclosed (+1) → BIMODAL
  φ: 17 symmetric (+1), 3 partial (0), 10 asymmetric (-1) → SKEWED SYMMETRIC

**Problem 1: No transient signs.** Every real script has simple signs (1-2 strokes).
These are ALL medium-to-complex. Real alphabets have 'I', 'O', 'L' alongside 'W', 'M', 'Q'.
This set has no simple signs. That's decorative, not functional.

**Problem 2: Too many symmetric signs.** 17 of 30 (57%) are bilaterally symmetric.
Real scripts average 20-35% symmetric signs. High symmetry = designed for visual
appeal, not information density. Symmetric signs are redundant — they waste half
their visual space mirroring the other half.

**Problem 3: No obvious base+modifier system.** Real scripts (Indus, Linear A,
Cypro-Minoan) have base signs with systematic modifications (added strokes, dots,
circles). These 30 signs share occasional elements (circles, arrows, lines) but
not SYSTEMATICALLY. There's no "this is sign X with modifier Y" pattern.

**Problem 4: The grid itself.** Real scripts aren't presented in grids unless
they're sign lists. But this grid has no visible ordering principle — not by
complexity, not by stroke type, not by visual similarity. A real sign list
would cluster similar signs together (all the circle-based signs, all the
arrow-based signs). This one doesn't.

### Verdict

**This is not a writing system.** (Confidence: 95%)

It's a set of 30 decorative geometric symbols designed to LOOK alien.
The tells:
1. No simple signs (everything is 3+ strokes)
2. Excessive symmetry (57% vs ~25% in real scripts)
3. No systematic modification patterns
4. No ordering principle in the grid
5. No text to analyze — just a symbol inventory with no syntax

A real alien language with 30 signs would be a syllabary (like Linear A with
45-70 signs). It would have simple AND complex signs. It would have a
base+modifier architecture. And most importantly, it would be USED — arranged
in sequences that show word structure, positional preferences, and combinatorial
constraints.

This has none of that. It's clip art.


## But If We're Having Fun...

If we READ the grid left-to-right, top-to-bottom as a single message,
the coordinate walk goes:

  (0,-1,-1,+1) → (0,+1,-1,-1) → (+1,0,-1,0) → ...

The walk oscillates in χ (left-right-left) and shows no convergence.
No spiral, no return to origin, no accumulating pattern. Random walk.

Compare to the Phaistos Disc, which shows clear register separation
(Side A prayer vs Side B narrative), or Linear A tablets, which show
accounting structure (items → totals). This grid shows... nothing.

If it IS a message, it says: "look at these cool shapes."

Which, to be fair, IS what VectorStock is selling.
