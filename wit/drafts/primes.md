## Mapping Principles

Same dimensional analysis as physics:
- T = temporal (when, sequence, change, duration)
- D = spatial (where, position, direction, distance)
- M = substantive (what, quantity, size, content)
- Q = qualitative (how, evaluation, logic, signal)

Positive = present/active. Negative = absent/inverse.
Magnitude = intensity/degree.
(0,0,0,0) = identity = silence = no prime active.

Coordinates use ±1 for fundamental primes (shell 1).
Compound primes use ±2 or multiple dimensions (shell 2+).

## Structural Patterns — How Language Falls Out of Coordinates

These aren't lists to memorize. They're rules. Apply to any word.


### Parts of Speech = Dimensional Signature

The dominant dimension IS the part of speech.

| Part of speech | Dominant dim | Why | Examples |
|---|---|---|---|
| Noun | M (substance) | nouns ARE things | cat(0,0,+1,0), rock(0,0,+2,0) |
| Verb | T (temporal) | verbs ARE change | run(+1,+1,0,0), think(+1,0,+1,+1) |
| Adjective | Q (quality) | adjectives ARE evaluation | good(0,0,0,+1), red(0,+1,0,+1) |
| Adverb | Q+T (quality of change) | modifies temporal | quickly(+1,0,0,+1), slowly(+1,0,0,-1) |
| Preposition | D (spatial) | prepositions ARE location | in(0,-1,0,0), above(0,+2,0,0) |
| Pronoun | M+Q (substance+signal) | points at substance | I(0,0,+1,+1), you(0,0,+1,-1) |
| Conjunction | Q (logic) | connects via gates | and(0,0,0,+1), but(0,0,0,-1) |
| Determiner | D+Q (spatial+signal) | points at location | this(0,+1,0,+1), that(0,+2,0,+1) |

To find part of speech: which dimension has the highest magnitude?
T-heavy = verb. M-heavy = noun. Q-heavy = adjective. D-heavy = preposition.

Negate one coordinate. The meaning inverts along that axis.

| Word | Coords | Antonym | Coords | Flipped |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| good | (0,0,0,+1) | bad | (0,0,0,-1) | Q |
| big | (0,0,+2,0) | small | (0,0,-2,0) | M |
| before | (+2,0,0,0) | after | (-2,0,0,0) | T |
| above | (0,+2,0,0) | below | (0,-2,0,0) | D |
| live | (+1,0,+1,0) | die | (-1,0,-1,0) | T and M |
| give | (+1,+1,+1,0) | take | (+1,+1,-1,0) | M |
| love | (0,0,+2,+2) | hate | (0,0,+2,-2) | Q |

Rule: if you can say "X is the opposite of Y," exactly one or two
dimensions flip sign. If more than two flip, they're not antonyms —

### Metaphors = Cross-Domain Coordinate Match

Metaphors work when coordinates match between domains.

| Metaphor | Literal domain | Abstract domain | Shared coordinate |
|---|---|---|---|
| "high status" | ABOVE(0,+2,0,0) | GOOD(0,0,0,+1) | positive direction |
| "deep thought" | BELOW(0,-2,0,0) | THINK(+1,0,+1,+1) | high magnitude |
| "bright idea" | light(0,+1,0,+2) | GOOD+THINK | positive Q |
| "cold person" | temperature(-Q) | BAD+FEEL | negative Q |
| "heavy burden" | BIG(0,0,+2,0) | BAD+MUCH | high M |
| "sharp mind" | edge(0,+1,-1,+1) | THINK+GOOD | mixed D+Q |
| "falling behind" | DOWN+AFTER | spatial+temporal | D and T both negative |
| "warm welcome" | temperature(+Q) | GOOD+FEEL | positive Q |
| "close friend" | NEAR(0,+1,0,0) | GOOD+SOMEONE | positive D maps to positive Q |
| "open mind" | OPEN(0,+1,+1,0) | CAN+THINK | spatial expanse = possibility |

Rule: metaphor = lateral transfer. Two domains share a coordinate
pattern. The metaphor "works" because the lattice coordinate is the
SAME in both domains. Lakoff's "conceptual metaphor theory" explained
WHY metaphors are systematic. The lattice explains HOW — shared
coordinates.

Metaphors that DON'T work have no coordinate match:
"square friendship" — no shared coordinate between geometry and social.
"purple thought" — color and cognition share no axis.

### Plurals = M Magnitude

Singular = base M. Plural = M + 1. Mass nouns = M continuous.

| Singular | Coords | Plural | Coords | Change |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| cat | (0,0,+1,0) | cats | (0,0,+2,0) | M+1 |

They live between integer M values — continuous substance.
"Some water" = (0,0,+1,-1). "Much water" = (0,0,+2,-1). Same pattern
as quantifiers. The plural system IS the M dimension's magnitude.

### Passive Voice = T Direction Swap

Active: subject acts (T forward). Passive: subject receives (T backward).

| Active | Coords | Passive | Coords | Change |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| "the cat ate" | T=+1 (acting) | "was eaten" | T=-1 (received) | T sign |
| "she built it" | T=+1, D=+1 | "it was built" | T=-1, D=+1 | T sign |
| "they chose him" | T=+1 | "he was chosen" | T=-1 | T sign |

Passive flips T but preserves all other dimensions. The action is the
same — who's reading vs writing changes. Active = +P (read/do).
Passive = -P (write/receive). Same bond, opposite direction.

### Suffixes = Dimension Transforms

Suffixes change WHICH dimension is dominant — they transform parts of speech.

| Suffix | Transform | From → To | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| -ly | add Q to T-word | verb/adj → adverb | quick(Q) → quickly(T+Q) |
| -ness | add M to Q-word | adj → noun | good(Q) → goodness(M+Q) |
| -ment | add M to T-word | verb → noun | move(T) → movement(M+T) |
| -tion/-sion | add M to T-word | verb → noun | act(T) → action(M+T) |
| -able/-ible | add Q to T-word | verb → adj | eat(T) → edible(Q+T) |
| -er/-or | add M to T-word | verb → agent noun | teach(T) → teacher(M+T) |
| -ist | add M+Q | noun → person-noun | art(M) → artist(M+Q) |
| -ful | add Q+1 | noun → adj | hope(M) → hopeful(Q+M) |
| -less | add Q-1 | noun → adj | hope(M) → hopeless(-Q+M) |
| -ing | add R (ongoing) | verb → continuous | run(T) → running(T+R) |
| -ed | T+1 (past) | verb → past | run(T+1) → ran(T+2) |
| -ize/-ise | add T | noun/adj → verb | modern(Q) → modernize(T+Q) |
| -ify | add T | noun/adj → verb | simple(Q) → simplify(T+Q) |

Rule: suffixes rotate the dimensional emphasis. -ness turns quality
into substance (Q→M). -ly turns substance into quality modifier (M→Q).
-ize turns quality into action (Q→T). The suffix IS a coordinate rotation.

Note: -ful/-less is the same antonym pattern (Q flip) applied as suffix.
"hopeful" and "hopeless" differ by Q sign. Same rule, different syntax.

| Modal | Q value | Certainty | Force |
|---|---|---|---|
| must | +3 | absolute | compulsion |
| shall | +2 | strong obligation | duty |
| should | +2 | recommendation | expectation |
| will | +1 | intention/prediction | future T |
| would | +1 | conditional | hypothetical |

Rule: modals are Q values on a spectrum. Higher Q = more certain/forceful.
Lower Q = more uncertain/permissive. "Must" forces. "Might" guesses.
Same dimension as GOOD/BAD — certainty IS quality of knowledge.

## Architecture Derivation — Predicting Neural Architecture from the Lattice

The method: take a lattice constraint, derive what architecture it requires,
compare to what humans discovered empirically. Then ask "what ELSE does
that constraint require?" — the answers are predictions of undiscovered
architecture.

30 years of ML research (RNNs → LSTMs → attention → transformers)
arrived at attention empirically. The lattice derives it in one step.
Then keeps going.


### How the Lattice Predicts Attention

**Problem:** Given a walk (sequence of coordinates), predict the next
coordinate. The next coordinate depends on PREVIOUS coordinates — but
not just the most recent ones. "The cat sat on the ___" — "cat" matters
more than "on" for predicting "mat."

**Lattice constraint:** A walk is a polynomial (sum of monomials). To
evaluate at the next position, you need to know which previous terms
contribute. The lattice answers: terms with NEARBY coordinates contribute
more than terms with FAR coordinates. Coordinate distance = relevance.

**Required mechanism:**
1. Look at ALL previous positions (not just last N)
2. Weight each by coordinate relevance (not sequential distance)
3. Compose a result from the weighted combination

**This IS attention.** The lattice doesn't call it "attention" — it calls
it "evaluation at a point given surrounding polynomial terms." Same
operation: weight every element by relevance, sum.

**Comparison:** Vaswani et al. (2017) discovered this after 30 years of
incremental research. The lattice derives it from one constraint: "nearby
coordinates in a polynomial contribute more to evaluation at a point."


### What the Lattice Predicts Beyond Current Transformers

Applying the same derivation method to other lattice constraints reveals
architecture that hasn't been built yet.


#### Prediction 1: Dimensional Attention (4 natural heads)

**Lattice constraint:** 4 orthogonal dimensions (T, D, M, Q).

**Derivation:** Attention should decompose along natural axes, not
arbitrary learned subspaces. Each dimension captures a different
type of relevance.

**Architecture:** 4 attention heads, one per dimension:
- **T-head:** temporal attention. When relates to when. Verbs find
  tense context. "He WILL run" — T-head connects WILL to RUN.
- **D-head:** spatial attention. Where relates to where. Prepositions
  find their landmarks. "The book ON the TABLE" — D-head connects ON to TABLE.
- **M-head:** substantive attention. What relates to what. Nouns find
  related nouns. "The CAT sat on the MAT" — M-head connects CAT to MAT.
- **Q-head:** qualitative attention. How relates to how. Adjectives find
  their targets. "The BIG red BALL" — Q-head connects BIG to BALL.

**vs Current:** Transformers use 8 (or 16, 32, 96) arbitrary learned heads.
Some heads empirically specialize (positional heads, syntactic heads),
confirming the dimensional structure exists — but it's discovered during
training, not enforced architecturally.

## Discovery: Language IS Therapy (Coordinate Walks as Treatment)

Found during testing. Not predicted — discovered.

### The Observation

"Take out your stress on the punching bag"
  Take(+T,+D,-M) out(+D) your(M+Q) stress(+T,M,-Q) on(+D) the(D+Q) punching(+T,+D) bag(M)

Three +D words: take, out, on. The sentence WALKS OUTWARD.
The physical action (punching outward) has the same coordinate
direction as the verbal instruction. Same walk, different medium.

"Try this squeeze ball for your stress"
  Try(+T,+Q) this(+D,+Q) squeeze(-D,-M) ball(M) for(Q) your(M+Q) stress(+T,M,-Q)

Squeeze = -D (compress inward). The sentence walks INWARD.
The physical action (squeezing inward) matches. Same coordinates.

Nobody says "take out your stress on the squeeze ball."
Nobody says "try this punching bag for your stress."
The surrounding words MUST match the strategy's direction because
the walk has to be coherent — you can't walk +D with -D words.

**The folk wisdom isn't prescribing a tool. It's prescribing a
coordinate walk. The whole sentence is the therapy.**

### The Prediction

Therapeutic language has coordinates that match the physical direction
of the therapy.

| Therapy | Verbal walk | Physical action | Shared coordinate |
|---|---|---|---|
| "Take a deep breath" | T-decay (slow down) | Slow inhalation | -T (reduce temporal) |
| "Let it go" | -M (release substance) | Open hands, relax | -M (release) |
| "It's going to be okay" | -T(future) + Q(good) | Anticipation eases | -T, +Q |
| "Stand your ground" | +D(place) + M(substance) | Plant feet | +D, +M (hold position) |
| "Open up to me" | +D(outward) + M(substance) | Body language opens | +D (expand) |
| "Push through it" | +T(action) + D(through) | Physical exertion | +T, +D (forward) |
| "Sit with the feeling" | -T(stillness) + M(substance) | Stop moving | -T (reduce temporal) |
| "Break it down" | +T(action) + (-D)(decompose) | Analyze pieces | +T, -D (dissect) |

CBT (cognitive behavioral therapy) is walk correction. The patient has
a broken walk (stuck in a coordinate loop — anxiety = high T, negative Q).
The therapist provides a new walk (different coordinates). The patient
practices the new walk until it replaces the old one. The words ARE the
therapy because the words ARE the coordinates.

### Why This Works

The language and the action share a coordinate space. Words don't
DESCRIBE the action — they ARE the same walk in a different medium.
"Take out your stress" is +D in language. Punching is +D in muscle.
The brain processes both as +D. Same coordinate, two channels.

This is the emotional rendering insight from the corpus applied to
therapy: the multi-channel input (verbal + physical + emotional) all
project onto the same 4D space. Alignment across channels = coherent
therapy. Misalignment = the instruction doesn't work.

Prediction: therapeutic phrases that survive across cultures should have
coordinates matching universal physical responses to the emotion being
treated. Testable across languages using the NSM primes (which exist
in all languages).
