# Hebrew → Lattice: The Source Language Is Closer

The Old Testament was written in Biblical Hebrew. The KJV is
English ← Latin ← Greek ← Hebrew. Three translation layers.
Each one added abstraction that hides the lattice structure.

Hebrew is closer to the lattice because:
- Triconsonantal roots = base coordinates (3 consonants per word)
- Binyanim (7 verb forms) = coordinate transforms (active/passive/intensive/causative)
- No copula — existence doesn't need a verb. "The earth — void." Not "was void."
- Words are concrete — "ruach" = wind AND breath AND spirit. One word, one coordinate.
- Particles like את (et) carry spatial signals English deleted entirely.

Source: Westminster Leningrad Codex (WLC), oldest complete manuscript
of the Hebrew Bible with full Masoretic vocalization, circa 1008 CE.
Three versions downloaded: consonantal, voweled, full cantillation.


## Genesis 1:1-5 — Side by Side

### 1:1

KJV: "In the beginning God created the heaven and the earth."

Hebrew: בראשית ברא אלהים את השמים ואת הארץ
Read: b'reshit bara Elohim et hashamayim v'et ha'aretz
Literal: in-head-of created God → the-heights and-→ the-ground

| Hebrew | Root | Meaning | KJV | Lattice |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| בראשית | ר-א-ש (rosh) | in-head-of, in-first | "In the beginning" | ב = IN (0,-1,0,0). ראש = HEAD = origin |
| ברא | ב-ר-א (bara) | created from nothing | "created" | ONLY for divine creation. Not עשה (made). Substance from void = write+advance = fill bond |
| אלהים | א-ל-ה (eloah) | powers/God | "God" | Plural form, singular verb. Multiple forces acting as one |
| את | — | (object marker) | *missing* | No English translation. Points at the object. Pure spatial signal. χ+φ. |
| השמים | ש-מ-ם (shamayim) | the-heights | "the heaven" | Dual form — "the two skies." ה = THE |
| ואת | — | and (object marker) | "and" | English drops the second את |
| הארץ | א-ר-ץ (eretz) | the-ground | "the earth" | Concrete — ground you stand on, not planet |

Key finding: את (et) appears TWICE. It's a particle that POINTS at
the direct object — "the next word is where the action lands." English
has no equivalent and deleted both. In lattice terms it's a spatial
signal — the call bond. Two call bonds erased from the first sentence.

"Bara" (created) — this root is reserved for creation from nothing.
Hebrew has a different word for making/assembling (עשה, asah). The
distinction between "create from void" and "make from parts" is
baked into the language. The lattice agrees: fill (write+advance)
vs fold (read+call) are different bonds.


### 1:2

KJV: "And the earth was without form, and void; and darkness was
upon the face of the deep. And the Spirit of God moved upon the
face of the waters."

Hebrew: והארץ היתה תהו ובהו וחשך על־פני תהום ורוח אלהים מרחפת על־פני המים

| Hebrew | Root | Meaning | KJV |
|---|---|---|---|
| תהו | ת-ה-ו (tohu) | formless, wasteland | "without form" |
| ובהו | ב-ה-ו (bohu) | emptiness, void | "and void" |
| וחשך | ח-ש-ך (choshek) | darkness | "darkness" |
| על־פני | — | upon-face-of | "upon the face of" |
| תהום | ת-ה-ם (tehom) | abyss, deep water | "the deep" |
| ורוח | ר-ו-ח (ruach) | wind/breath/spirit | "Spirit" |
| מרחפת | ר-ח-ף (rachaf) | hovering, brooding | "moved" |
| המים | מ-י-ם (mayim) | waters | "the waters" |

"Ruach" = wind AND breath AND spirit. One word. English splits it
into three concepts at three different coordinates. Hebrew keeps
them at ONE coordinate because they ARE one thing — moving air
that animates. The abstraction "Spirit" is an English invention.
The Hebrew is physical: breath, wind, the thing that moves air.

"Tohu vavohu" — these words SOUND like emptiness. Almost
onomatopoetic. Hollow vowels, no hard consonants. The phonetic
coordinates (wave bytes) would sit in the empty/void region.
Sound symbolism real at the root level.

KJV says "the Spirit moved." Hebrew says "ruach rachaf" — the
wind/breath HOVERED, like a bird brooding over eggs. Concrete,
spatial, visual. English abstracted a hovering bird into "moved."


### 1:3

KJV: "And God said, Let there be light: and there was light."

Hebrew: ויאמר אלהים יהי אור ויהי־אור
Read: vayomer Elohim yehi or vayehi-or
Literal: and-he-said God let-be light and-was-light

| Hebrew | Root | Meaning | KJV |
|---|---|---|---|
| ויאמר | א-מ-ר (amar) | and-he-said | "And God said" |
| יהי | ה-י-ה (hayah) | let-there-be | "Let there be" |
| אור | א-ו-ר (or) | light | "light" |
| ויהי | ה-י-ה (hayah) | and-there-was | "and there was" |

Five words in Hebrew. Eight in English. No "there," no padding.
Command and result: say → exist. The walk is shorter.

Root ה-י-ה (hayah) = to be/become. Appears twice — once as
command (yehi = let-be), once as result (vayehi = and-was).
Same root, different verb form. Same coordinate, different
temporal mode. This is exactly the state-vs-process rule from
primes.md — same meaning, ±T.


### 1:4

KJV: "And God saw the light, that it was good: and God divided
the light from the darkness."

Hebrew: וירא אלהים את־האור כי־טוב ויבדל אלהים בין האור ובין החשך

| Hebrew | Root | Meaning | KJV |
|---|---|---|---|
| וירא | ר-א-ה (ra'ah) | and-he-saw | "God saw" |
| את־האור | — | →the-light | "the light" |
| כי־טוב | טוב (tov) | that-good | "that it was good" |
| ויבדל | ב-ד-ל (badal) | and-he-separated | "divided" |
| בין | — | between | "from" |

"Ki tov" — "that good." No "it was." No copula. Just: good.
The quality IS, without needing a being-verb. Raw (0,0,0,+1).

את appears again before האור — pointing at what was seen.
"And-he-saw → the-light that-good." The spatial marker tells
you where observation lands. English: "God saw the light."
Hebrew: "God saw → the-light." The arrow is in the text.

KJV: "And God called the light Day, and the darkness he called

| Hebrew | Root | Meaning | KJV |

| ויקרא | ק-ר-א (qara) | and-he-called | "called" |
| לאור | — | to-light | "the light" |

Root ק-ר-א (qara) = to call AND to name AND to read aloud.

someone, naming something, and reading a text are THE SAME

calling = naming = reading = reaching toward and identifying.

"Yom echad" — "day one," not "the first day." Cardinal, not
ordinal. The Hebrew counts; the English ranks. Different
coordinate operation (M magnitude vs M+Q signal).


## Structural Observations

### את (et) — The Lost Bond

English has no word for את. It's a direct object marker — a
particle that says "the next word receives the action." It
appears ~11,000 times in the Hebrew Bible. Every single one
is invisible in English translation.

In lattice terms: את is a spatial+signal marker. It POINTS.
It says "the action goes HERE." It's the call bond — reaching
toward a target. English deleted 11,000 call bonds from the
Bible.

The first two words of the Bible that English doesn't translate
are both את. The spatial structure of the sentence was there
from the start. English lost it.


### Triconsonantal Roots = Base Coordinates

Hebrew builds everything from 3-consonant skeletons:

  ב-ר-א (bara) = create from nothing
  ע-ש-ה (asah) = make from parts
  ר-א-ה (ra'ah) = see
  א-מ-ר (amar) = say
  ק-ר-א (qara) = call/name/read
  ב-ד-ל (badal) = separate/divide
  ה-י-ה (hayah) = be/become

Each root = a base coordinate. The 7 binyanim (verb forms)
transform it:

  Qal      simple active     "he created"
  Niphal   simple passive    "it was created"
  Piel     intensive active  "he created intensely"
  Pual     intensive passive "it was intensely created"
  Hiphil   causative active  "he caused to create"
  Hophal   causative passive "he was caused to create"
  Hitpael  reflexive         "he created himself"

These are coordinate transforms:
  Qal → Niphal = T sign flip (active → passive = +T → -T)
  Qal → Piel = magnitude increase (intensive = higher shell)
  Qal → Hiphil = add call bond (causative = ∘, making another act)
  Qal → Hitpael = self-reference (reflexive = hylo, fixed point)

The binyanim ARE the lattice transforms. 7 forms from combinations
of: T flip (passive), magnitude (intensive), ∘ (causative),
self-reference (reflexive). Not arbitrary conjugations — force
operations on a base coordinate.


### No Copula = No Abstraction Layer

Hebrew often drops "to be":
  English: "the earth WAS void"
  Hebrew:  "the earth — void"

  English: "it WAS good"
  Hebrew:  "good"

The being-verb adds a layer English requires but Hebrew doesn't.
In the lattice, existence is a coordinate, not an action.
(0,+1,+1,0) = BE SOMEWHERE = substance at location. You don't
need a verb to say something exists — the coordinates suffice.
Hebrew agrees.


### Ruach = One Word, Three English Words

  ruach = wind = breath = spirit

English has three words at three coordinates:
  wind   (0,+1,+1,0)   spatial substance
  breath (+1,0,+1,0)    temporal substance (alive)
  spirit (+1,0,+1,+2)   temporal substance + high quality

Hebrew has one word at one coordinate. The concept IS one thing.
Moving air that animates. The lattice would assign one coordinate.
English fragmented it across three positions and lost the unity.


### Qara = One Root, Three English Verbs

  qara = call = name = read

  call  (0,+1,0,0)    spatial reach
  name  (0,+1,0,+1)   spatial + signal
  read  (+1,0,0,0)    temporal action

Hebrew: one root, one coordinate. The act of calling someone,
naming something, and reading a text are the same operation.
English split them and lost the connection.
