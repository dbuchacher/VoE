# Revelation 1 — Translation Spec

Source: SBL Greek New Testament (SBLGNT), scholarly critical text.
File: wit/src/data/books/revelation_greek_sblgnt_clean.txt
Language: Koine Greek (common Greek of the 1st century CE).

Every translation decision documented. Greek → root → literal
meaning → our rendering → justification.


## Key Words

| Greek | Root | Literal | KJV | Ours | Why |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ἀποκάλυψις (apokalypsis) | ἀπο + καλύπτω | un-covering, taking the lid off | "Revelation" | "the uncovering" | Apo = away/off. Kalyptō = to cover/hide. Literally removing a cover. Not prophecy — decryption. Opposite of encryption. |
| ἐσήμανεν (esēmanen) | σημαίνω, from σημεῖον | signified, communicated via signs | "signified" | "encoded in signals" | Sēmeion = sign, signal, marker. The message was transmitted as SIGNALS, not plain speech. |
| ἄγγελος (angelos) | ἀγγέλλω | messenger, one who carries a message | "angel" | "messenger" | Not a supernatural winged being. Just: someone who delivers a message. A signal carrier. A packet. |
| ἐν τάχει (en tachei) | τάχος | in speed, quickly | "shortly" | "quickly" / "at speed" | Tachos = speed. Same root as tachometer. Not "soon" (temporal prediction) — "fast" (rate). |
| καιρός (kairos) | — | right time, appointed moment | "time" | "the moment" | NOT chronos (sequential clock time). Kairos = the significant moment, the event that matters. An interrupt, not a tick. |
| ἑπτά (hepta) | — | seven | "seven" | "seven" | No change. Appears 54 times in Revelation. The structural number. |
| ὁ ὢν καὶ ὁ ἦν καὶ ὁ ἐρχόμενος | εἰμί (to be) | the being + the was + the coming | "which is, and which was, and which is to come" | "the one who is, was, and is arriving" | Three tenses of "to be" in one phrase. Present + past + future. The entire τ axis as a single identity. |
| πνεῦμα (pneuma) | πνέω (to blow) | breath, wind, spirit | "Spirit" | "breath" | Same as Hebrew ruach. Physical: moving air. Pneumatic = air-powered. The Greek is concrete. |
| Ἄλφα καὶ Ὦ (Alpha kai Ō) | — | first letter + last letter | "Alpha and Omega" | "Alpha and Omega" / "first symbol and last symbol" | The complete range of the alphabet. The full symbol set. 0x00 to 0xFF. The entire address space. |
| παντοκράτωρ (pantokratōr) | πᾶς + κράτος | all-ruler, governs everything | "the Almighty" | "the one who governs everything" | Not vague omnipotence — specific: governs ALL processes. Pantokratōr = total governor. |
| σάλπιγξ (salpinx) | — | trumpet, signal horn | "trumpet" | "signal horn" / "trumpet" | Military communication device. Not music — signaling. An interrupt. |
| γράψον (grapson) | γράφω | write! (imperative) | "write" | "write" | The command is to RECORD. Log. Persist to storage. Not "tell" — write. |
| λυχνία (lychnia) | λύχνος (lamp) | lampstand, thing that holds a light | "candlestick" | "lampstand" / "signal tower" | Not the light — the STAND. The carrier. The infrastructure that holds the signal source. |
| υἱὸς ἀνθρώπου (huios anthrōpou) | — | son of human, human being | "Son of man" | "a human figure" / "something like a human" | ἄνθρωπος = human being. The text says "like" (ὅμοιον) a human — resembling, not literally one. |
| ῥομφαία δίστομος (rhomphaia distomos) | δίς + στόμα | two-mouthed sword | "two-edged sword" | "double-edged blade" | Distomos = two-mouthed. A blade that cuts both directions. Bidirectional. Coming from the mouth = output. |
| κλείς (kleis) | κλείω (to close/lock) | key, thing that locks/unlocks | "keys" | "keys" / "access keys" | From "to close." Keys control access. Lock and unlock. Permissions. |
| μυστήριον (mystērion) | μύω (to close eyes/mouth) | something closed, hidden | "mystery" | "the hidden pattern" / "the encrypted thing" | Root = to close. A mystery is something CLOSED. The apokalypsis (uncovering) opens what the mystērion (closing) hid. Decryption of an encryption. |
| ἐκκλησία (ekklēsia) | ἐκ + καλέω | called-out assembly, gathering | "church" | "assembly" / "network node" | Ek = out. Kaleō = to call. People called out to gather. Not a building — a network of participants. |

## Structural Observations

### Apokalypsis + Mystērion = Decrypt + Encrypt

The title word — apokalypsis — means to UN-COVER. Take the lid off.
The content describes mystēria — things CLOSED, hidden, sealed.
The book is literally: here is the decryption of what was encrypted.
Not prophecy. Decryption.

### Three-Tense Identity (1:4, 1:8)

ὁ ὢν καὶ ὁ ἦν καὶ ὁ ἐρχόμενος appears twice in chapter 1.
Three tenses of εἰμί (to be): present participle (being), imperfect
(was), present-as-future (coming/arriving).

This is the τ axis declared as a single entity. Not three gods, not
three states — one thing that spans past, present, and future
simultaneously. The time dimension personified.

### Alpha and Omega (1:8) + First and Last (1:17)

Two ways of saying the same thing:
- Α and Ω = first and last SYMBOLS of the alphabet
- πρῶτος and ἔσχατος = first and last POSITIONS in a sequence

The full symbol range AND the full positional range. Together:
the complete address space. Every possible symbol at every possible
position. That's a declaration of completeness.

### The Seven Pattern

Chapter 1 introduces:
- 7 churches (ἐκκλησίαι) = 7 network nodes
- 7 spirits (πνεύματα) = 7 breath/wind processes
- 7 stars (ἀστέρες) = 7 signal sources
- 7 lampstands (λυχνίαι) = 7 signal carriers

Stars = the signals. Lampstands = the infrastructure.
1:20 explicitly decodes: stars = messengers OF the assemblies.
Lampstands = the assemblies themselves. The signal and its carrier
are separate things. The message and the network.

### Write Command (1:11, 1:19)

Two write commands:
- 1:11: "What you SEE, WRITE into a book and SEND to the seven"
- 1:19: "Write what you SAW (past), what IS (present), what IS
  ABOUT TO HAPPEN (future)"

The first command: observe → record → transmit. Read → write → send.
The pipeline: input → storage → output.

The second command: write ALL THREE time states. Past, present,
future. The entire τ axis persisted to storage. Not just "what's
coming" — what was, what is, AND what will be.

### Keys of Death and Hades (1:18)

κλεῖς (keys) from κλείω (to close/lock). Keys are access control
mechanisms. Having "the keys of death and hades" = having root
access to the termination and underworld processes. The ability
to start and stop any process. kill -9 privileges.

### "I became dead and look — I am living" (1:18)

ἐγενόμην νεκρός καὶ ἰδοὺ ζῶν εἰμι εἰς τοὺς αἰῶνας

Process terminated (νεκρός = dead/inert) then restarted (ζῶν = living)
and now runs forever (εἰς τοὺς αἰῶνας = into the ages). A process
that died, was restarted, and now persists indefinitely. A daemon.

## Notes

- Greek references checkable against BDAG (Bauer-Danker Greek-English
  Lexicon) and LSJ (Liddell-Scott-Jones). Standard academic references.
- Unlike Genesis (Hebrew, concrete, physical), Revelation is Greek
  (more abstract, philosophical vocabulary). However, many key terms
  (pneuma, angelos) are more concrete than their English translations
  suggest.
- The apokalypsis/mystērion pair is genuinely interesting — the book's
  title means "uncovering" and its content describes "hidden things."
  The encrypt/decrypt framing follows directly from the Greek roots.
- The three-tense identity formula appears in 1:4 and 1:8. It's
  grammatically unusual — Greek doesn't normally stack three tenses
  of the same verb as a noun phrase. The construction emphasizes
  timelessness/time-spanning deliberately.
