# Heat Death Dissolved

The heat death prediction extrapolates a local law to infinity.
The ring has no infinity. It has depth.


## The Standard Claim

Entropy always increases (2nd law). Universe reaches maximum entropy.
No gradients. No work. Everything dies. Inevitable.

Assumes: unbounded, one-directional time. No structural limit.


## The Lattice

Heat death depends on damping policy:

  drop:   write cursor laps read → overwrite oldest → recycle
  block:  write cursor hits depth → stop → equilibrium → death
  kick:   reject new writes → backpressure → collapse

Only block produces heat death. Drop makes it structurally impossible.


## Why Drop

Evidence for drop damping:

  - Expansion accelerating (empty slots pulling harder, not decelerating
    toward a wall — block would show deceleration)
  - 75% of ring still empty (68% dark energy + dark matter headroom)
  - No rejection signal (kick would show contraction)
  - Observed universe shows no sign of approaching a capacity limit

Drop = the system is designed to keep writing, keep cycling.


## Second Law Becomes Local

Within any read-to-write window: entropy increases. Older records
are more disordered relative to newer. This is real. The 2nd law
holds locally.

But the ring wraps. Globally, entropy oscillates — oldest
high-entropy records get overwritten by new low-entropy structure.
The monotonic increase is a property of the window, not the ring.

The arrow of time is real (write cursor always advances forward).
The content recycles (same substrate, new structure).


## Heat Death = Extrapolation Error

The 2nd law describes the overwhelmingly probable direction within
a local window. Extrapolating to "the universe dies" assumes:

  1. Time is unbounded (ring has finite depth)
  2. Time is one-directional (ring wraps)
  3. No recycling mechanism (drop damping IS the mechanism)

All three assumptions fail in a ring buffer universe.


## The Universe Doesn't Die — It Composts

Drop damping = cyclical universe. Not Big Bounce (collapse and
re-expand). The write cursor keeps advancing, wraps around,
overwrites the oldest regions. New structure on the same substrate.

Not eternal return (same content repeating). The write cursor
always advances — new records, new structure, same capacity.
The substrate is eternal. The content evolves.


## Predictions

  - No heat death observed at any scale (correct so far)
  - Local entropy increase always holds (correct — 2nd law works)
  - Expansion continues accelerating until write cursor approaches
    depth, then behavior reveals damping policy
  - If drop: distant regions show recycled structure (old overwritten,
    new emerged) — oldest observable light is from near the previous
    write-cursor wrap
  - CMB is the boundary: the oldest records still within the current
    read-to-write window. Beyond it = already overwritten.


## CMB as Ring Boundary

The cosmic microwave background is the oldest light we can see.
Standard: it's the surface of last scattering, ~380,000 years
after the Big Bang, beyond which the universe was opaque.

Lattice: it's the read cursor. Everything behind it has been
processed (read and overwritten in previous cycles, or simply
the allocation boundary of this cycle). The CMB's uniformity
(~2.725K everywhere, 1 part in 100,000 variation) is what you'd
expect at a cursor boundary — the edge of the current window,
smoothed by the wrap.
