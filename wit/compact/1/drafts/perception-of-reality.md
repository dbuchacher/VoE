What IS real, what does "look like" mean, and why the question
"are we in a simulation?" is malformed.

## The Sun Doesn't Look Like Anything

property of YOUR READER. Your retina maps incoming boson records to

telescope "sees" the same sun completely differently. Neither is wrong.

Looking = P (read). Appearance = how the reader's bond pipeline renders
the reads. The appearance lives at the read end, never at the source.

## "Simulation" Is a Broken Word

"Simulation" implies something less real — a copy, a model, a fake
that refers to a "real" original. The lattice says there is no "less real."

Computation IS physics (not metaphor — structural identity, 72 confirmed
correspondences). A ring buffer running bond rules IS a physical system.
Not a description of one. Not a model of one. The thing itself.

The question "are we in a simulation?" assumes a meaningful distinction
between "simulated physics" and "real physics." There isn't one. Both
are ring buffers running bonds. The only difference is which side of
the ring the observer is on.

From inside the ring, there is no experiment that distinguishes
"real" from "simulated." Not because the evidence is hidden — because

And they'd look out and wonder if THEIR universe was "real."

The asymmetry is epistemic, not ontological. It's about where the
reader sits, not about what's real.
